### Behavioral Archetypes and Synthetic Collective Logic in Social Modeling

Generative Agent-Based Modeling (GABM) often falls into a trap of behavioral homogeneity. When agents are defined only by identity profiles, such as age or occupation, they all tend to act the same way by posting original content too often. Recent work shows that adding a specific layer for behavioral traits fixes this issue. These traits define how an agent acts across the full action space of a platform, including posting, re-sharing, and reacting.

The framework uses seven archetypal profiles to regulate these actions. Silent Observers remain passive. Occasional Sharers only amplify content. Interactive Enthusiasts focus on likes and comments. Proactive Contributors prioritize original posts. Balanced Participants mix all actions. These profiles keep agent behavior consistent and prevent the simulation from collapsing into a single pattern.

At a different scale, the Synonymix pipeline moves simulations to the meso-level. This approach uses a unigraph to represent the shared history of a group. It breaks life stories into three node types. Subject nodes represent entities. Factual nodes represent events. Interpretive nodes capture the meaning people give to those events. By merging these into a single graph, researchers can study how groups build consensus or experience history differently. Theme anchors (vector embeddings of interpretive nodes) help the system sample new personas without creating stories that contradict themselves.

Analyzing actual platform behavior requires more than hashtags, which are often misleading. Video Content Analysis (VCA) uses vision language models to convert video and audio into mathematical vectors. This method allows for automated analysis of millions of videos at once. By clustering these vectors, researchers see that users often inhabit micro-bubbles of content on a daily basis but see a lot of variety over several months. These tools show that user interactions, like likes or shares, do not change future recommendations as fast as people think. Instead, the videos a user sees right before they like something have a stronger influence on their behavior.
